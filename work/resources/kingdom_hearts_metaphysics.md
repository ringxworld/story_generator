# Kingdom Hearts Metaphysical Model (Reference)

This note captures a thematic framework for story design and evaluation.

## Core model

### 1) What a heart is

A heart is the capacity to participate in shared meaning.

- Not emotion.
- Not morality.
- Not optimism.

It is the ability to let:

- connection bind you,
- meaning make claims on you,
- loss count as real.

A heart exists where participation is chosen despite risk.

### 2) Darkness (individual level)

Darkness is interior withdrawal for self-protection.

- It begins as boundary-setting.
- It becomes dangerous when it hardens into total disengagement.

Darkness is not evil. It is the cost of having a self.

### 3) Heartless (individual failure mode)

A Heartless is a state where:

- shared meaning is abandoned,
- reality itself is judged not worth inhabiting,
- participation is replaced with negation or anesthesia.

The person retreats into "unreality" where nothing can make demands.

Key point:

A Heartless does not hate the world. They conclude the world is ontologically invalid.

This is meaning collapse, not sadness.

### 4) Nobody (individual failure mode)

A Nobody is a state where:

- meaning is acknowledged abstractly,
- but denied to the self,
- function continues without interior claim,
- systems are maintained, but not inhabited.

Core belief:

"Meaning exists, but not for me."

This produces high-functioning alienation.

### 5) Princess of Heart (ideal psyche)

A Princess of Heart is defined structurally, not morally.

Psychological traits:

- identity is constituted by connection, not defended from it,
- interiority is not hoarded,
- meaning is allowed to circulate freely,
- presence stabilizes others without control.

They act as:

- narrative anchors,
- coherence nodes,
- load-bearing points for shared reality.

They are vulnerable because they do not privatize meaning.

### 6) World-level metaphysics

A world exists as long as its shared premise is inhabited.

A world falls to darkness when:

- enough participants withdraw,
- its meaning stops being binding,
- people stop choosing to stay.

Worlds do not fall because they are false. They fall because no one participates anymore.

### 7) Darkness (world level)

At scale, darkness is:

- unclaimed space,
- loss of commitment,
- fragmentation of shared belief.

Darkness fills vacuums left by disengagement.

### 8) Xehanort / Matrix logic (system-level failure)

Systems like Xehanort's world or the Matrix share a premise:

Awareness and creativity destabilize worlds, so they must be suppressed.

They attempt to:

- eliminate contingency,
- enforce unity or ignorance,
- make questioning impossible.

These systems achieve stability by abolishing authorship.

Kingdom Hearts rejects this premise.

### 9) Core claim

A world is only legitimate if:

- people can fully realize its contingency,
- understand it could be otherwise,
- and still choose to inhabit it.

Stability without choice is not success.

### 10) Quadratum framing

Quadratum represents:

- a reality where meaning is not enforced by narrative,
- participation is optional and unsupported,
- creation replaces immersion.

It is "author space" rather than "story space."

## One-paragraph summary

This model treats meaning as participatory rather than given. A heart is the capacity to participate in shared meaning despite risk. Darkness starts as self-protection and becomes destructive when it hardens into disengagement. A Heartless is global meaning collapse and retreat into unreality; a Nobody is continued function with denied personal participation. Worlds exist only while their shared premises are inhabited, and they fall when participation collapses at scale. Systems like Xehanort's or the Matrix try to preserve worlds by suppressing awareness and choice, but this model rejects that as illegitimate stability. Legitimate worlds must survive being understood, doubted, and still chosen.

## Optional fiction mappings

- Heartless: Obito (as Tobi), Sayaka (witch state)
- Nobody: Ayanokoji, early Hachiman
- Princess of Heart: Chitanda Eru (idealized), Kairi (structural role)
- Heartless-making system: Kyubey
- Forced coherence: Xehanort, Matrix Agents
- Author-space awakening: Quadratum
